Yellowstone National Park

  • Sits on a dormant volcano.
  • Home to more geysers and hot springs than any other place on earth.
  • Home to the famous geyser, Old Faithful.
  • Yellowstone spans almost 3,500 miles.

Big Bend National Park

  • 150 miles of hiking trails.
  • Comprised of 1,252 square miles of land, making it larger than the state of Rhode Island.
  • Established as a national park in June of 1935.
  • Home to 1,200 species of plants and 450 species of birds.

Hawai'i Volcanoes National Park

  • established in 1916.
  • Designated as an International Biosphere Reserve in 1980 and a World Heritage Site in 1987.
  • In addition to Mauna Loa, the park includes Kilauea, the world's most active volcano.
  • Over half of the park is designated wilderness.
